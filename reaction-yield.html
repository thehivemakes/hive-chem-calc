How to Calculate Reaction Yield

Theoretical yield is the maximum amount of product that could be formed from a given amount of reactant, assuming 100% conversion. It is calculated from stoichiometry.

Percent yield compares actual yield to theoretical yield: % yield = (actual / theoretical) × 100. Yields above 100% indicate impurities or measurement error. Typical lab yields range from 40–90% depending on the reaction.
